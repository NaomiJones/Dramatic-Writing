What was your major? ( Was it difficult for you to decide? Did you end up changing it? Did it end up being helpful for jobs? Are you satisfied with it, if not, what would you prefer?)

"My major was journalism but I switched to media communications. I had this deadest vision that I was going to be a sports journalist and marry a basketball player, that’s not why I chose the major but definitely about 75% of the reason. I always knew why I chose it but after taking classes like script writing, broadcast media, and video editing I realized I wanted to be more behind the scenes instead of in front of the screen. I was really good at editing and I had the time to do the internships I wanted I definitely think I would have been satisfied. To this day I still try to use the skills I learned in school even though I'm in a whole different field.”

"My major changed from Art to business and now Cyber security. Due to the nature of my journey my major will provide stability and will lend to my credibility but it was not a requirement for my job. I do believe that my major will help me prepare for the next chapter of my life which is somewhat unknown, but having a degree gives me the confidence that I will be okay.”

"I started school undecided and quickly changed to a Spanish major since I enjoyed the language. I was never a school fanatic, so I went with what I enjoyed and quickly changed that to a dual major with business to incorporate my dreams of success. Eventually as my personal business grew I explored technology and fell in love with the field. My curiosity, dedication and passion allowed me to grow quickly. Today, I am a computer science major in my senior year and also a senior Developer for a fortune 500 corporation.”
