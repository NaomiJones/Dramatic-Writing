How has the concern of money impacted your college experience?

"If you stay dedicated to cafe food you’ll be ok, but ordering out all the time will be brutal to your bank account. For tuition money is literally the end, all be all. If you can’t afford tuition then don’t do it to yourself. I’m 18,000 in debt because I gambled thinking I could get enough scholarships and grants and couldn’t ...now I can’t even get my transcripts to go back to school, so it’s kind of like I kid for school to never actually get the credit that I was in school. So get scholarships, get grants, and if it’s a full ride at a school you weren’t hoping to go, still do it, masters are really what matters anyway.”

"I was fortunately enough to qualify for grants and Student loans. Although its never good to have debt, I was not one of those students that left school with $50,000+ in loans.”

"The concern of money greatly impacted the beginning of my college experience. I had to leave my original dream school with all my friends and the life of a college basketball player. I left Berry because I couldn’t afford private school tuition and did not have the guts to rack up six figures worth of loans. At Kennesaw, money was less of an issue since taking out loans was less daunting and today I am fortunate enough to be able to afford those gambles.”
