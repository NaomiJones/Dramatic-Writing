What was your major? ( Was it difficult for you to decide? Did you end up changing it? Did it end up being helpful for jobs? Are you satisfied with it, if not, what would you prefer?)

“Sociology. I was originally a social work major. It was in the social science field, so it is kind of helpful for the job I have now, but I worked at a movie theater and Forever 21 for two years. I should have chosen a business major but I was afraid of taking math.”

“I always knew growing up that I wanted to be an Accountant, just like my aunt. I would see her come home from work all dressed up and carrying a suitcase and I wanted to be just like her. I was also very into math and was very good at it. My Accounting degree opened many doors for me, I was able to move to GA from NY and find a job within two weeks. I’m currently pursuing my MBA and CPA. Eventually, my goal is to open my own Bookkeeping and tax services company.”

“I graduated with a major in Psychology and a minor in Anthropology. It wasn’t difficult for me to decide and I did change it a few times. I started college as a Biology major on the pre-med track, I switched to Neuroscience because I wanted more of a cognitive approach. I realized with how difficult those classes were and my lack of interest in the classes, that I did not want to be on the pre-med track. Once I made that decision I majored in Psychology. It’s proven extremely helpful with jobs, having that degree opens a lot of opportunities, specifically within the UI/UX field. Yes, I am satisfied with the major I chose because I plan to obtain more degrees in the future and it paved the way for me to do so.”
