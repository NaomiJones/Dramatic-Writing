Describe how much you’ve grown/developed throughout college, what was one major thing you’ve learned that affected you most?

"I only did about 4 semesters in college so 2 years, but I definitely did grow but mostly by the mistakes I made. First semester I was party girl, second semester I was all in my books and more focused on bringing my GPA up. When I left school the first time it was half way through the second semester and I was basically thrown into adulthood, I worked four jobs at the same time, went to beauty school because I thought it would be a better route, dropped out of that and I was confused and didn’t realize that my education was all I had unless I wanted to get stuck working a job $10 and lower. So I would say leaving school because I thought I could make it without it was my biggest mistake but also lead me to working harder on bettering myself.”

"What help me grow the most or what affected me the most about college was the opportunity to live in a world different from the neighborhood that I grew up in. I learned that there are thousands of people who work hard to pursue their dreams. I learned that I was one of those people.”

"I started college as a 170 pound 17 year old teen with dreams that didn’t extend past success and the NBA. Today I am a man establishing my business, starting my family and reaching the goals I set for myself back then, well at least most of them. I won't mention todays weight or how my hoop dreams don’t extend past the app 'Are They Hooping' that I created. If there was one thing that I learned that stuck with me throughout college it would be the importance of finding a passion and never giving up on that passion."
